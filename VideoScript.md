## Video Script

Slide 1  
My group's presentation today is about TraceMap, basically an AI powered food safety platform used by authorities all across the European Union. The main question it was built to solve is pretty simple: how do you keep food safe when it crosses through so many different countries before it even gets to your plate?

Slide 2 - What is TraceMap?
So what actually is TraceMap? At its core it's a software platform powered by AI and it's used by EU food safety officials like government agencies, health ministries, border control etc . Its whole job is to monitor the food supply chain and track products from the moment they're produced all the way to when they're sold to you. And if anything looks off like a contamination risk or a sketchy supplier it catches it before it turns into a big problem. Think of it like a radar system for food safety across Europe.

Slide 3 - Why Was TraceMap Created?
So why did something like this even need to exist? The EU's food supply chain is huge and super complex. Food gets sourced from all over the world and it's all so interconnected that trying to track it manually is basically impossible. Additionally  cases of food fraud were going up things like mislabeled products, fake ingredients and bacterial contamination. And the systems they had before were just way too slow and could only deal with a problem after it already happened. They needed something faster, smarter and able to catch problems before they spread.

Slide 4 - How Does TraceMap Work?
Here's how it actually works. First it collects data from existing EU systems mainly RASFF which handles food safety alerts and traces which tracks trade movements. Then AI algorithms go through all that data looking for unusual patterns the kind of thing a human inspector would probably miss. When something suspicious gets spotted it gets flagged automatically. And then authorities get a real time alert so they can act on it right away. The whole thing runs on its own which makes  it way faster and more consistent than doing it by hand.

Slide 5 - Key Features
A few things that stand out about Tracemaps are: It does end to end traceability so every product every single step of the way is tracked. It can identify high risk suppliers and unusual shipping routes that might signal something's wrong. It has automated risk detection and sends out real time alerts. It also supports cooperation between EU countries which is really important when you've got 27 member states all involved. And it has a visual dashboard with mapping tools so officials can literally see on a map where an issue is coming from.

Slide 6 - Real World Impact
As for the real world impact, food recalls happen a lot faster now inspections are more accurate and the whole approach has gone from reacting to problems to actually preventing them. It also connects all the EU member states into one unified safety network instead of everyone running their own separate system. And for regular people it just means you can actually trust that the food you're buying has been watched and monitored the whole way through.

Slide 7 - Why TraceMap Matters
So beyond all the features why does this actually matter? It protects public health. It stops food scandals before they blow up. It builds trust between consumers and the big food companies they buy from. It makes supply chains way more transparent than they usually are. And honestly it's just a really good example of AI being used for something genuinely useful in government. Something other countries could realistically look at and copy.

Slide 8 - Conclusion
In conclusion TraceMap is more than just a tracking tool. It's a system that's quietly working in the background every day to make sure the food supply stays safe for hundreds of millions of people. A couple personal takeaways that I got from researching this programme is that ; AI used in this manner can actually be an extremely reliable resource. And it's always better to prevent a problem than to deal with it after the fact. Hence the the consistent usage of this software
